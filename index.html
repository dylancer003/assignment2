Emotional Support Animals and their benefits

Information on the benefits of Support Animals

Learn More

Social and Emotional Boost

Smiles and tail wags aren’t just cute—they’re clinical. Human-animal interactions (HAI) stimulate the release of oxytocin, a hormone that enhances trust, empathy, and prosocial behavior. Studies show that children with autism and psychiatric disorders demonstrate increased social awareness, playfulness, and communication when engaging with animals like dogs or horses.

Mental Health Support

Animal-assisted therapy reduces anxiety and depression by lowering cortisol levels—the body’s primary stress hormone. Controlled studies show that even brief interactions with therapy animals can significantly elevate mood, reduce loneliness in elderly populations, and improve motivation and emotional balance in those with schizophrenia or PTSD.

Better Physical Helath

Physiological benefits of pet interaction include lowered heart rate, reduced blood pressure, and improved heart rate variability. In cardiovascular patients, the presence of a dog during stress tests led to faster recovery. One study even showed hypertensive patients experienced a 50% drop in cardiovascular reactivity after acquiring a pet.
